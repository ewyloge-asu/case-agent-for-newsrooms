Agent of Record

It works the case. People write the story.

A records-investigation toolkit for newsrooms that use AI coding agents. It handles the slow parts — loading messy public records, connecting name variants to real people and companies, finding outliers, tracking leads across weeks of sessions, checking claims against the actual law, footnoting a draft to primary sources — while keeping every number traceable to the record it came from.

One boundary is built in: the agent does not write the story. It does the records work and hands your reporter material they can verify. News judgment, framing, and the writing stay with people.

01 · The tool

What it is

Six Agent Skills that work as one pipeline, following the same four steps a careful reporter would:

1 · Scope the questionThe agent interviews you: what's the question, which entities, what time frame, what counts as a finding? That scope becomes the case file's brief and picks the right tools for the beat. Click for details +
What's actually happening
  • Your answers become two small files: a plain-English brief — the investigation's charter — and a settings file that points the rest of the tools at your beat.
  • Lobbying comes pre-configured. Any other beat gets a starter plan built from your answers: what to treat as an entity, where to look for outside data, what counts as unusual.
  • If the data later suggests a bigger or smaller story, the agent asks again, and the change is written down.
Click to flip back
2 · Gather & analyzeDeterministic scripts load the records into a database, resolve messy names to real entities, profile what's normal, and surface what isn't — including searching the web for outside datasets and APIs to check claims against. Click for details +
What's actually happening
  • Scripts read the raw records once and organize them into a small database on your computer — a year of federal lobbying filings takes about 15 seconds.
  • Name cleanup connects the variations — "Rep. Brett Guthrie," "GUTHRIE, BRETT," "Guthrie for Congress" — to the same real person, so the money adds up correctly.
  • A data-finder suggests public datasets and official sources for your topic, and anything you pull in is saved with a digital fingerprint so it can be re-checked later.
Click to flip back
3 · Stay organizedA case file on disk tracks every thread (open, chasing, confirmed, or dead), every key entity, and a session journal — so next week's session picks up exactly where this one stopped. Click for details +
What's actually happening
  • The case file is a folder of ordinary text files — a brief, one file per lead, a list of who matters, a diary of what happened each session. You can read or edit any of it without special software.
  • Built-in rules keep it honest: a lead can't be marked confirmed without a source record behind it, and a dead end has to say why it died.
  • One command turns the whole case file into a dashboard page an editor can open in any browser.
Click to flip back
4 · Harden every claimBefore anything goes to an editor: read the actual statute and apply it element-by-element, footnote the draft to primary sources as tracked changes, and flag what still needs a human decision. The tool prepares claims for review; publishing judgments belong to people. Click for details +
What's actually happening
  • The legal checker quotes the actual statute and walks through it requirement by requirement. The strongest thing it will ever say is "possible — ask a lawyer."
  • The footnoter attaches each claim in a draft to its source, as a tracked change in Word — and where it can't find support, it says so in a margin note rather than inventing one.
  • Any number, anywhere in the work, can be traced back to the exact government filing it came from with one command.
Click to flip back

Underneath all four steps, one rule: every number carries a provenance ID that traces back to the raw record it came from, so an editor can audit any claim in one click.

02 · The skills

The tools

An Agent Skill is a folder of instructions and scripts that a coding agent (Claude Code, Cursor, Cowork) picks up automatically. Each of the six works on its own; together they're the pipeline above. Get them by cloning the repo — each card links to its folder.

Start here: the conductor

investigative-method

Interviews you to scope the investigation, opens the case file, and drives the other five skills in order. Includes tools that find public datasets and official APIs for any topic.

View the skill

Find the leads

lobbying-influence-mapper

The analysis engine, configured for money-in-politics records: loads filings into a database, resolves messy names to real people and companies, ranks who's paying whom, and checks it against outside records. The pattern generalizes to other beats.

View the skill

Understand any table

robodoig

Point it at any CSV or spreadsheet and get a profile: what the columns mean, what's normal, what's missing, what correlates — before anyone trusts a number. Named for Steve Doig, the Pulitzer-winning ASU data-journalism pioneer.

View the skill

Never lose the thread

case-file

The investigation's memory: every lead's status, every key entity, a session journal — plain text files your whole desk can read. Come back a week later and the agent re-orients itself instead of starting over.

View the skill

Is it actually illegal?

checking-the-law

Takes a lead, pulls the real statute text, and applies it element-by-element — never from memory. Its verdicts are deliberately cautious: "possible," "unclear, ask a lawyer," or "not a violation, and here's why."

View the skill

Source every claim

howard-center-footnoter

Reads a draft story, matches each factual claim to your reporting materials, and inserts footnotes as Word tracked changes — with margin comments where no source exists or a quote may be out of context.

View the skill

03 · Fifteen minutes

Get started

You need Python 3.9+ and either a code-executing AI agent or just a terminal. Every skill ships a small demo dataset, so nothing else is required for a first run. Full walkthrough: getting-started.md.

Claude Code (desktop app or the claude CLI)
  1. Clone the repo: git clone https://github.com/ewyloge-asu/case-agent-for-newsrooms.git
  2. Copy the skills in: cp -r case-agent-for-newsrooms/skills/* ~/.claude/skills/
    (one project only: .claude/skills/ inside that project instead)
  3. Ask: "Use the lobbying-influence-mapper skill on its demo data: ingest, resolve entities, and show me the top recipients." You should have a ranked, sourced leaderboard in under a minute.
  4. Then start yours: "Use the investigative-method skill. I want to investigate [your question] using [where your records live]."
Claude Cowork
  1. Clone or download the repo and add the folder to your Cowork workspace.
  2. Cowork sessions with code execution can run every script. If your records live on your machine, mention their path in your first message.
  3. Ask: "Read skills/investigative-method/SKILL.md and follow it to start an investigation."
Cursor & other agents that support Agent Skills
  1. Clone the repo and copy skills/* into your agent's skills directory (Cursor: ~/.cursor/skills/), or open the repo folder as part of your workspace.
  2. Any agent without skills support works with one instruction: "Read skills/investigative-method/SKILL.md and follow it." The SKILL.md files are the manual.
No agent — plain terminal

The deterministic pipeline runs by itself, on the bundled demo data or your own records:

# demo data (bundled); point GAIN_DATA_DIR at your own records instead when ready
cd skills/lobbying-influence-mapper
export GAIN_DATA_DIR=$PWD/demo_data GAIN_WORKDIR=/tmp/casework
python3 scripts/ingest.py --years 2025 --datasets senate contributions press
python3 scripts/resolve_entities.py
python3 scripts/xref.py gatekeeper --filer lobbyist --year 2025 --top 10

# profile any table before trusting it (needs: pip install pandas numpy matplotlib openpyxl)
python3 ../robodoig/scripts/analyze.py your_table.csv /tmp/profile

# find public datasets and official APIs for any topic
python3 ../investigative-method/tools/find_data.py "your topic"

Standards & safety

Two documents your editor and your counsel will want before this touches a story — the short versions:

Editorial standards

docs/editorial-standards.md

Why the no-story-writing boundary is a design decision; how every anti-hallucination property is mechanical rather than promised: deterministic scripts do the math, the footnoter flags instead of filling, the legal checker cannot say "illegal," and a lead can't be marked confirmed without a source record attached.

Read the standard

Data & source protection

docs/data-and-source-protection.md

Everything the scripts do stays on your machines — no server, no account. What leaves: whatever your AI agent reads goes to its model provider. The rules we'd set: public records freely; confidential source material away from cloud agents unless your org has real zero-retention terms.

Newsroom workflow

Who touches what: the reporter drives in chat, the editor reads a generated dashboard, counsel gets element-by-element legal memos, the fact-checker gets the footnoted draft. Plus what a realistic first project looks like.

05 · Generality

Any beat with records

The method — scope, ingest, profile, find what deviates, track it, harden it — is not tied to one dataset. The scoping step builds a starter plan for whatever beat you name, the data-finder locates public sources for it, and the profiler takes any table. We've exercised the full loop on federal lobbying records and on Medicare hospital billing data; procurement and contracts are the natural next beats. Where this is going, and how to bend it toward your beat today: the roadmap.
